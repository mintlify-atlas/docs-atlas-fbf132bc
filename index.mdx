The QuPath StarDist Extension brings the power of the [StarDist](https://github.com/stardist/stardist) deep learning model directly into [QuPath](https://qupath.github.io), enabling accurate 2D nucleus and cell detection on whole-slide images. Whether you're working with H&E-stained tissue, fluorescence microscopy, or immunohistochemistry, StarDist provides robust star-convex polygon detections with a clean, scriptable Java/Groovy API.

## What is StarDist?

StarDist detects cell nuclei by predicting star-convex polygons — a shape family that naturally describes most nuclei. Each nucleus is represented as a set of radial distances from its centroid to its boundary, enabling accurate detections even in densely packed tissue. The method was introduced by Schmidt et al. in ["Cell Detection with Star-convex Polygons"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.03535) (MICCAI 2018).

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the extension">
    Download the latest `qupath-extension-stardist-[version].jar` from [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/qupath/qupath-extension-stardist/releases) and drag it onto the QuPath window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Obtain a StarDist model">
    Download a pre-trained model such as `he_heavy_augment.pb` for H&E images, or train your own model using the Python StarDist library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a detection script">
    Open the QuPath Script Editor, select one of the bundled StarDist templates from **Extensions > StarDist**, and configure the model path and parameters for your image type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run and explore results">
    Execute the script to detect nuclei across your selected annotations. View detections in the QuPath viewer and export measurements for downstream analysis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The StarDist extension requires QuPath v0.5.0 or later. See the [changelog](/changelog) for version-specific compatibility information.
</Note>
